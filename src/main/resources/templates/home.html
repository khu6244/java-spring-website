About Me
Hello! My name is Kevin Hu and I am a second-year Engineering Science student at the University of Toronto. This is a web application that I have developed over roughly a month during the summer between my first and second years of university. This was my first excursion into the world of Java web applications and various relevant frameworks. I wanted to gain some relevant programming experience as I thought that the introductory programming courses that all Engineering Science students are required to take were not sufficient. Additionally, our heavy courseload gives us little latitude to pick electives until we specialize in third year. I was inspired by the websites that some people make to showcase their work and thought I could make a website while gaining some experience with full-stack development.

A complete description of my work and academic experiences can be found in my resume or LinkedIn, but here is a short summary of how I got here. I took the International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme at Guelph Collegiate Vocational Institute, picking Chemistry, Language/Literature, and History as my Higher Level courses while taking Standard Level Math, Physics, and ab initio Spanish. I wrote my Extended Essay in History and completed all my IAs, TOK, and CAS requirements to earn an eventual 44 points out of a possible 45. The point I missed out on was in Spanish. We were not able to take our exams at the end of our two years of IB due to COVID, but I don't particularly regret or resent the missed opportunity to earn a perfect IB score as it didn't have any relevance to me after February of 2020 when I was accepted into Engineering Science at the University of Toronto, my first choice program.

A quick word on my extracurriculars in high school. I got involved in executive positions and creative extracurriculars where I could, but I mainly participated in sports. I loved, and still do love, playing sports. In high school, I played four varsity sports a year: tennis in the fall, wrestling in the winter, and both badminton and rugby in the spring. Now, I can only find the time to play recreationally, but my health and fitness are important things to me so I will always find the time to do something.

I took my first year of Engineering Science fully online due to COVID, which was certainly a tough adjustment but in many ways made the transition to university easier. I was at home for the fall term and only moved out in winter term, so I had a two-part university initiation that smoothed my adjustment. In many ways, I was extremely lucky compared to many of my peers and many people worldwide. I got involved in Robotics for Space Exploration (RSX) in my first year, joining the electrical subteam and learning about circuits and Altium. I completed first year successfully and with a fairly high mark, which now brings me to the summer after first year and where I am now.

During the summer of 2021, I decided to undertake a few passion projects. I had wanted to make a custom mechanical keyboard from scratch for about a year now but did not have the time to do so during the school year, so I started and saw that through to completion at the beginning of the summer. The second project I wanted to try was a woodworking project. I made a coffee table with my dad in our garage as I went home for about half the summer and it now resides in the living room of my Toronto apartment. Finally, I made this website to display information about myself as well as learn about and practice full-stack development. I learned much from each of these projects and I am content with what I have completed this summer.

I now head into my second year of Engineering Science (reputed to be at least as tough as first year). I think that I have more highly developed time management skills and I have learned how to survive tough courseloads, but it will still be a challenge that makes me nervous just thinking about. My goal is to complete my second year to at least the same degree of success as my first year while enjoying my time on-campus, and balancing my extracurricular activities (which now include a co-directorship of another club). If this About Me page, my GitHub, and this website as a whole are not being updated constantly, hopefully you can infer something about the direness of my "drowning in textbooks" situation.
